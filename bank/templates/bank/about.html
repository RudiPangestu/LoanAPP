What is a Bank?

A bank is a financial institution licensed to receive deposits and make loans. Banks may also provide financial services such as wealth management, currency exchange, and safe deposit boxes.

Key Functions of a Bank:
  • Accepting Deposits: Banks safeguard money for individuals and businesses.
  • Providing Loans: Banks lend money to borrowers for various purposes.
  • Money Transfer: Banks facilitate the transfer of funds between accounts.
  • Financial Advice: Banks offer guidance on financial matters and investments.

Banks play a crucial role in the economy by facilitating the flow of money, supporting economic activity, and helping individuals and businesses achieve their financial goals.
